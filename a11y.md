## My Commitment

I want everyone to be able to use this site — no matter what device, assistive tech, or level of ability you're working with. The aim is to meet the [WCAG 2.2 AA standards](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/), and while we may not be perfect yet, I'm always working on it.

## What Might Not Be Fully Accessible (Yet)

Here’s where I know things could be better:

- Interactive visualizations may not be fully keyboard-friendly.
- The contrast ratio of some elements may not meet the desired 7:1 ratio

Any accessibility changes are tagged on Github, so you can track what’s improving.

## How We Review Accessibility

Accessibility is checked using a mix of automated tools (Like WAVE, ARC Toolkit and SiteImprove), manual testing, and feedback from real users. This statement is revisited at least once a year — or whenever I make big changes.

## Need Help or Want to Share Feedback?

If you’ve hit a barrier or need something in a different format, I’d love to hear from you:

- Submit an issue to our [issue tracker on GitHub](https://github.com/stickerboy/stickerboy.github.io/issues/new)
- By email: [a11y@kenny.cx](mailto:a11y@kenny.cx "Send me an email")

## If Things Don’t Get Resolved

I do my best to make this site accessible and respond to feedback quickly.  
If you’re not happy with how I’ve handled an accessibility issue, you can contact the official accessibility support service in your country.

In the UK, that’s:

**Equality Advisory and Support Service (EASS)**  
[https://www.equalityadvisoryservice.com](https://www.equalityadvisoryservice.com)
